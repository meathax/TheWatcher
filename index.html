Tier 1

Polish — existing features made premium

Six fixes that take v2.0 systems from 70% great to 100% landing.
#1

Real signature weapons

When you kill a hunted leader, their dropped weapon is registered in a per-save dictionary with a generated name like "Reaver of Vega" or "Vega's Last Word".

A Harmony patch on Thing.LabelNoCount renames the weapon in every UI; another on Thing.GetInspectString adds the slain leader's lore ("Taken from Warlord Vega of the Sons of Vega. Killed by Brennick on day 87.").

The fix: a Pawn.Kill Prefix captures the weapon reference BEFORE vanilla drops it, so the Postfix can register it reliably.

#2

Physical artifact trophies

Six new ThingDefs — Watcher's Tablet, Crown of Vega, Mercury Ledger, Healer Codex, Royal Archive, Starmap Fragment — drop at the colony as museum-piece items when their artifact is recovered, in addition to the persistent effect.

Players have tangible loot from every artifact, not just an invisible buff.

#3

Starmap visions feed the Watch List

The Starmap Fragment artifact previously granted nothing the player could see. Now it adds 3 "Watcher's vision" predictions to the Atlas's Watch List, sourced from long-tail signals: planned successors, fastest-rising faction, high-resentment imminent wars.

Each fragment recovered stacks an additional 3 predictions.

#4

Rich battle-scar narratives

Every dropped scar's scarDetails is enriched at drop time with a multi-paragraph narrative: named leaders at the time of the event, casualty estimates scaled by scar type, atmospheric closer ("The journal ends mid-sentence. Whoever was writing did not live to finish.").

When excavated as a "war journal" find, that's what the player reads — actual lore, not a tombstone.

#5

Walking pursuer caravans

Refugee pursuit now spawns a real hostile Caravan with 3-5 pawns walking the world map toward the refugees. Re-paths daily toward the refugees' current tile.

Player can see the pursuer caravan's ETA on the world map and intercept it directly. Tile-equality catch instead of a hidden timed roll — real time pressure.

#6

Hunt gizmo defender preview

The "Hunt {Title} {Name}" gizmo tooltip now shows estimated defender threat points, the leader's top 3 traits, top 2 priorities, and grievance count.

You can plan loadout before committing instead of marching in blind.

Tier 2

Content depth — more meaningful play hours

Six features that reuse existing data to make the long-arc simulation feel dramatic.
#7

Successor faction personality

When a faction collapses and a successor emerges, the new FactionDef is now different from the predecessor's whenever possible — a Pirate collapse may produce an Outlander successor, creating real tonal shift on the world map.

The new faction also gets a 60-day "isolationist" window: warier, less likely to declare war, finding its feet.

#8

Vendetta-tier escalation

At 5/10/20 raids from the same faction, the Watcher recognises a vendetta and fires a campaign letter. Every faction hostile to the vendetta-faction now treats your colony with +15/+30/+45 goodwill bias.

Hunting the vendetta-faction's leader becomes a priority Watch List entry. Personal narrative compounding.

#9

Succession crisis window

When any visible leader dies (via your hunt or natural death), their faction enters a 7-14 day crisis: synthetic Falling power trend, atlas-tracked, more vulnerable to player attack.

Connects assassination to genuine geopolitical opportunity — a real window where the faction is vulnerable.

#10

Anti-player alliance bloc

When two factions both have Resentment ≥ 60 with player AND mutual Respect ≥ 40, they form an "Alliance against the colony" bloc. Campaign-defining endgame threat.

Dissolves only by destroying one of them OR shifting the long-term political map enough that one no longer resents you.

#11

Refugee survivor stories

Welcomed refugees are now tagged with a survivor flag referencing their origin settlement, and receive a mood thought on joining ("Gratitude" or "NewColonyOptimism").

Their backstory connects to your campaign's history — they remember where they came from.

#12

Combat-encounter scars

30% of ancient WarBattle scars (18% for other types) now generate a combat encounter map instead of a dialog loot roll. Defenders scaled to caravan strength via Medium tier (~1.0× points).

Surviving the ambush yields a heavy haul: 800-1500 silver + 40-80 plasteel + 6-14 components. Bigger payoff, real risk.

Tier 3

UX polish — clarity and discoverability

Four small fixes with disproportionate impact on player experience.
#13

Dev triggers hidden by default

F12 → EventInjector force-trigger buttons are now gated behind a showDevTriggers mod setting (off by default).

Production players don't see "Force-spawn outpost" / "Force-collapse faction" anymore. Testers can enable them in mod settings.

#14

Atlas filter pill bar + search

The Atlas's filter row is redesigned as clickable category pills (Settlement, Outpost, War, Refugee, Pirate, Successor, Leader, Scar, Other) plus a faction dropdown plus a free-text search box.

The Atlas is now browsable instead of greppable.

#15

Leader inspect detail

Settlement inspect strings now show: "Home of Warlord Vega" + "Known for: Bloodlust, Cautious, Pyromaniac" + "Driven by: Vengeance" + "Holds 4 grievances against your colony".

You get intelligence on a leader before deciding to hunt them.

#16

Schema version warning

WatcherSchemaVersion = 21 is scribed on the WorldComponent. When loading a save made with an earlier version of The Watcher, the player gets a Log.Warning noting the mismatch — preventing silent data confusion.

Tier 4

Signature — features that define the mod

Three ambitious additions that change what The Watcher feels like to play.
#18

The Watcher's Voice

Every ~30 days (25% roll), a first-person message from the Watcher reflects on your campaign. Composed from a pool of openings, factual middle reflections, and atmospheric closers — pulling from your recent collapses, the most-resentful faction, your colony's archetype, atlas event count.

Sample: "It is day 87. I have been watching since before you arrived. I remember the Sons of Vega. They were a power once, and you watched them fall. You have shaped this colony into a Fortress. I know what that means for who you will become. Carry on. — The Watcher"

The storyteller stops being a system. It becomes a character.

#19

Endgame Faction Recognition

At day 200+ with sufficient renown (composite of all faction-disposition magnitudes), the Watcher offers to recognise your colony as a peer faction on the world map.

Accept: +20 goodwill with your three best-disposed factions, NewColonyOptimism mood for all colonists, Atlas badge marking the colony as a recognised faction.

Decline: −6 goodwill with every faction — they read your refusal as cowardice.

A campaign-defining decision, one shot.

#20

Visible cultural drift

Settlements of factions whose CulturalDrift.TotalDrift ≥ 30 now show their adoption of your colony's culture directly in the inspect string:

"Visibly influenced by your colony's culture (ideology)."
"Has heavily adopted your colony's culture (technology drift dominant)."

Players see the long-tail cultural simulation producing change on the world map.

"I am not always cruel. I am never asleep."
